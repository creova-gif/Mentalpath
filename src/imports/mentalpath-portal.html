Informed consent & privacy
Please read and sign before your first session. This is a legal requirement under PHIPA and College of Registered Psychotherapists of Ontario (CRPO) guidelines.

Consent to Services & Privacy Notice

By proceeding, you acknowledge and agree to the following:


Nature of Services. Psychotherapy involves a collaborative process to address emotional, psychological, and relational concerns. While therapy can be highly beneficial, outcomes cannot be guaranteed. You have the right to ask questions about the therapeutic process at any time.


Confidentiality. All information shared in therapy is strictly confidential. Your therapist will not disclose your personal information to any third party without your written consent, except where required by law: (a) if there is a serious and imminent risk of harm to you or others; (b) if there is reasonable suspicion of child abuse or neglect; (c) if records are subpoenaed by a court of law.


Your Privacy Rights (PHIPA). Under the Personal Health Information Protection Act (Ontario), you have the right to: access your personal health information; request corrections to your record; withdraw consent at any time (with some limitations); and file a complaint with the Information and Privacy Commissioner of Ontario.


Data Storage. Your personal health information is stored securely on Canadian servers (AWS ca-central-1, Montreal/Toronto). It is encrypted at rest using AES-256 and in transit using TLS 1.3. Your data will never be shared with US authorities or stored outside Canada.


Fees & Cancellation. Sessions are billed at the rate agreed upon with your therapist. Cancellations made less than 24 hours in advance may be subject to a late cancellation fee. Your therapist will discuss fee details with you directly.


Records Retention. Your clinical records will be retained for a minimum of 10 years from the date of your last session, as required by PHIPA s.20 and College guidelines.
