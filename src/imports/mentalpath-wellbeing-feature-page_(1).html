Why this exists

Every other tool in this space is designed to make therapists more productive, more organised, more compliant.

None of them stop and ask how the therapist is doing. And therapists — who spend their entire working lives asking other people that exact question — almost never get asked it themselves.

Compassion fatigue and vicarious trauma are occupational realities in this profession. The research is unambiguous: therapists who don't monitor their own emotional load have worse clinical outcomes, higher burnout rates, and are more likely to miss things in session.

The College of Registered Psychotherapists of Ontario actually includes self-care as an ethical obligation in their Standards of Practice.

So we built a private, weekly check-in that lives only for the therapist. Not visible to clients. Not in the audit log. Not exportable. Never shared.

What it does

Everything you track for your clients. Finally for you.

01
Wellbeing scores
Four sliders each week. Energy. Emotional load. Satisfaction. Boundary clarity. Plain language, not clinical scales. Just how it actually felt.
02
Vicarious trauma indicators
Six flags mapped to the validated ProQOL-5 and Figley compassion fatigue model. Toggle what you're noticing. The system escalates its language at 4+ flags.
03
Private reflection
A textarea that explicitly tells you it's never read by anyone. No share button. No export. No audit entry. Just a place to write what's sitting with you.
04
AI reflection back
Not advice. Not a to-do list. Not cheerfulness. A trusted-supervisor voice that names what the week held, notices one thing, and offers one possibility. Tone shifts based on what you enter.
05
Self-care tracking
Did you have supervision this week? Take intentional breaks? Attend personal therapy? Six checkboxes. No judgment. Just the data so you can see your own patterns.
06
Your history over time
Are heavy weeks clustering? Does emotional load consistently outrun energy? Does supervision actually move the needle for you? For the first time, you have data about yourself.

This isn't wellness theatre. It's the same rigour therapists apply to their clients — applied to themselves.

Session dread
Feeling reluctance or anxiety about seeing a particular client — more than just a difficult case
Emotional numbing

Cynicism increase
Noticing skepticism about whether therapy helps, or feeling jaded in ways that feel unfamiliar
Presence difficulty
Mind wandering, struggling to hold attention, feeling like you're watching yourself rather than being there

Genuinely private.
Not just a promise.

We made architectural choices to enforce this, not just policy choices. The `therapist_checkins` table has its own RLS policy — `therapist_own_checkins_only` — that's stricter than anything else in the system.

It cannot be joined to client data. It doesn't appear in audit logs. It cannot be exported. It cannot be seen by MentalPath staff. Technically impossible, not just against the rules.

Row-level security — own account only
Database-enforced. Your check-ins are only ever returned to a query authenticated as you. No exceptions, no overrides.
Not in the audit log
PHIPA requires we log access to client health records. Your personal check-ins are not health records. They generate no audit entries.
No export, no share, no supervisor view
We deliberately did not build these. Not a settings toggle. Not a future feature. Never.
Stored in Canada with everything else
AWS ca-central-1, Montreal & Toronto. AES-256 at rest. TLS 1.3 in transit. Same infrastructure as your most sensitive clinical data.
